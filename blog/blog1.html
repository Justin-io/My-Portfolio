The Dawn of Agentic AI

Jan 08, 2025 • Hari Nandan K • 5 min read

The era of passive chatbots is ending. We are witnessing the rise of Agentic AI—systems that don't just generate text, but actively reason, plan, and execute tasks using tools.

Beyond the Chatbox

Traditional LLMs like GPT-4 are powerful reasoning engines, but they are trapped in a text box. Agentic workflows break these chains by giving the model "hands"—access to APIs, file systems, and the web.

The J.A.R.V.I.S. M1 Architecture

My project, J.A.R.V.I.S. M1, explores this paradigm. By using a local hybrid core (Ollama), it processes voice commands and translates them into system-level actions on Linux. It doesn't just say "I can do that"—it runs the command.

Key components of an Agentic System:

  • Reasoning Loop: The brain (LLM) decides what to do next.
  • Memory: Short-term context and long-term vector stores.
  • Tools: Functions the model can call (calculator, web search, bash).

The Future

As we move into 2025, the prompt engineer will evolve into the Agent Architect. The skill lies not in asking the right question, but in building the right environment for the AI to succeed.
