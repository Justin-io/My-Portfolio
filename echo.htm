About the Workshop

What is ECHO ?

ECHO refers to complex behaviors and capabilities that arise in artificial intelligence systems that weren't explicitly programmed. This workshop explores how these phenomena occur and how we can harness them through effective prompt engineering.

emergent properties in AI systems include self-organization, adaptation, and the ability to solve problems in ways not anticipated by their creators. These properties emerge from the complex interactions between simpler components, much like how consciousness emerges from neural networks in the human brain.

In this workshop, you'll learn to recognize, understand, and leverage these emergent properties to create more powerful and flexible AI applications.

Prompt Engineering

Learn the art and science of crafting effective prompts to elicit desired responses from AI systems. Master techniques to optimize AI outputs for various applications, from creative writing to complex problem-solving.

Prompt engineering is both a science and an art. It requires understanding how AI models process language, recognize patterns, and generate responses. You'll learn about context setting, role assignment, and how to structure prompts for maximum effectiveness.

We'll cover advanced techniques like chain-of-thought prompting, few-shot learning, and prompt chaining to handle complex tasks that require multiple steps of reasoning.

AI Agents

Discover how to build and deploy with AI agents that can perform tasks autonomously. Understand agent architectures, decision-making processes, and how to create agents that adapt to new environments and challenges.

AI agents represent the next evolution in artificial intelligence - systems that can perceive their environment, make decisions, and take actions to achieve specific goals. You'll learn about different agent architectures, from simple reflex agents to complex learning agents.

We'll explore how to build with agents that can work autonomously, collaborate with other agents, and adapt to changing environments - skills that are increasingly valuable in today's AI-driven world.

Workshop Curriculum

A hands-on, project-driven journey from AI fundamentals to building intelligent agents. Each module builds practical skills you can apply immediately.

01

Introduction to AI & Large Language Models

Build a strong foundation in artificial intelligence and understand how modern language models work under the hood.

• Evolution of AI: from rule-based systems to deep learning
• Core concepts in machine learning: supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning
• Neural networks and how they learn from data
• Architecture of Large Language Models (LLMs)
• Transformer models and self-attention mechanisms
• How models like GPT, Llama, and Claude generate text
• Limitations and capabilities of current AI systems
• Bias, fairness, and ethical challenges in AI
• Real-world impact across healthcare, education, and business

02

Prompt Engineering Fundamentals

Learn how to communicate effectively with AI models using structured, clear, and powerful prompts.

• How LLMs interpret and respond to prompts
• Essential components: instruction, context, input, and output format
• Setting roles and personas to guide AI behavior
• Writing precise, unambiguous instructions
• Using examples to improve output quality
• Common failure modes: hallucinations, vagueness, off-topic responses
• Prompt debugging and iterative refinement
• Hands-on practice with real AI tools
• Best practices for clarity, tone, and consistency

03

Advanced Prompt Techniques

Unlock the full reasoning power of AI with cutting-edge prompting strategies used by experts.

• Chain-of-thought prompting to improve logical reasoning
• Few-shot learning: providing examples to shape responses
• Zero-shot vs. one-shot vs. few-shot trade-offs
• Prompt chaining: breaking complex tasks into steps
• Tree-of-thought and graph-based reasoning methods
• Self-consistency: generating multiple paths and selecting best answer
• Handling ambiguous or incomplete queries
• Optimizing prompts for accuracy, creativity, or brevity
• Evaluating and benchmarking prompt performance

04

Building with AI Agents

Go beyond prompts and learn how to create autonomous AI agents that plan, act, and adapt.

• What is an AI agent? Definition and key components
• Agent architectures: reflex, goal-based, utility-based
• Perception, planning, action, and feedback loops
• Tools and APIs: enabling agents to interact with the world
• Memory systems: short-term and long-term recall
• Decision-making frameworks and reasoning engines
• Autonomous task execution and error recovery
• Case studies: customer support bots, research assistants, automation agents

05

Hands-on Projects

Apply your skills to build real AI-powered applications from concept to completion.

• Design and build a conversational AI assistant for personal use
• Create a research agent that gathers and summarizes web information
• Develop a task automation agent for scheduling or email drafting
• Build a creative writing assistant with genre and tone control
• Implement a code generation tool with error checking
• Integrate tools, memory, and prompts into a working agent
• Present your project and receive expert feedback
• Export and share your agent for real-world use

06

AI Safety, Evaluation & Deployment

Learn how to test, evaluate, and responsibly deploy AI systems in real environments.

• Evaluating AI outputs: accuracy, relevance, coherence
• Testing for bias, toxicity, and factual consistency
• Red teaming: identifying failure modes and edge cases
• Monitoring agent behavior in production
• Privacy and data security best practices
• User feedback loops and continuous improvement
• Deployment options: web apps, APIs, browser extensions
• Documentation and explainability for stakeholders

07

Future of AI & Career Applications

Explore emerging trends and discover how to leverage AI in your career or business.

• The rise of agentic workflows and AI teams
• AI in software development, marketing, research, and education
• Building AI-powered side projects and startups
• Integrating AI into your daily productivity stack
• Staying updated in a fast-changing field
• Building a portfolio with AI projects
• Networking with the AI community
• Career paths: prompt engineer, AI consultant, agent developer
• Resources for continued learning and growth

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need prior programming experience?

No prior programming experience is required. We start from the basics and gradually build up to more advanced concepts. All you need is enthusiasm and a willingness to learn! Our instructors are skilled at explaining complex concepts in simple terms, and we provide plenty of hands-on guidance throughout the workshop.

What tools/software do I need?

You'll need a laptop with a modern web browser. We'll provide access to all necessary AI tools and platforms during the workshop. No additional software installation is required. All the platforms we use are cloud-based, so you won't need to worry about system requirements or compatibility issues.

Will I receive a certificate?

Yes, all participants who complete the workshop will receive a certificate from HOPE Int Core recognizing their expertise in prompt engineering and AI agents. The certificate includes details of the topics covered and can be a valuable addition to your professional portfolio.

Is there any post-workshop support?

Absolutely! We provide ongoing support through our community forum, regular webinars, and access to updated learning materials. You'll be part of a growing AI community where you can continue learning, share projects, and get help with any challenges you face.

Can I get a refund if I can't attend?

We offer full refunds up to 7 days before the workshop. After that, we can transfer your registration to a future workshop or another participant. We understand that plans can change, and we try to be as flexible as possible while ensuring we can accommodate all participants.

How is this workshop different from other AI courses?

Unlike many AI courses that focus only on theory, our workshop emphasizes practical, hands-on learning. We focus specifically on prompt engineering and AI agents - cutting-edge areas that are in high demand but rarely covered in depth. Our instructors bring real-world industry experience, and our curriculum is designed to give you skills you can immediately apply.

How long is the workshop?

The workshop runs for two half-days or one full day, depending on the schedule. Each session is about 3–4 hours, with breaks. You’ll get hands-on practice, live demos, and time to ask questions all designed to fit your busy life.

Is there homework or assignments?

No required homework, but we provide optional practice tasks to reinforce your skills. These take 15–30 minutes and help you build confidence using AI tools in real-world scenarios. Participation is fully optional and self-paced.

Are sessions recorded?

No, sessions are not recorded. We encourage live participation to get the most out of hands-on activities and real-time Q&A. However, all slides, code samples, and key resources will be shared afterward so you can review the content at your own pace.

Can students attend?

Absolutely! Students from any field are welcome. The workshop is beginner-friendly and especially useful for those exploring AI careers, building projects, or boosting productivity in studies. Many students use it for internships or personal growth.

Will I work on real projects?

Yes! You’ll build a real AI-powered project during the workshop like an automated assistant or content generator. You’ll leave with something practical you can show, use, or even turn into a side hustle.

What if I’m in a different time zone?

We schedule sessions at times that accommodate major global regions. If live attendance is difficult, let us know we’ll help you catch up with shared materials and community support.
