A BIT ABOUT ME

I Like Building Things (And Figuring Out Why They Break)

I'm a B.Tech student who keeps asking "why?" about spacetime, about security, about why a kid in a village can't access a good tutorial. I don't always have answers. But I keep looking.

I'm fascinated by two things: how the universe works at the smallest scales, and how to build useful things with code. Right now I'm working on a personal research project I call the Chronon Model basically trying to simulate what spacetime might look like if it were made of tiny discrete pieces. It's very much a work in progress. I also build smaller, practical stuff like a data science portal for my college and a little e-commerce site called ShopRoyince.

I started a small initiative called HOPE it's basically me trying to combine a few things I care about: teaching, building, and helping students who don't have access to good resources. Over the past few years, I've been lucky enough to help around 430 students get their hands dirty with cybersecurity labs and Arduino/ESP32 projects.

Physics (The Deep End)

Exploring what spacetime might be made of at the smallest scales. The math is hard. The questions are harder. I love both.

Building Stuff

Websites, apps, and circuits that (mostly) work

Teaching (And Learning From Students)

Teaching students security and hardware. They keep me honest.

Breaking Things (Responsibly)

Finding vulnerabilities before they get exploited. IoT devices and web apps are my usual hunting ground.
