RESEARCH

Chronon-Super Quantum Level Model

Pioneering the future of quantum gravity theory

Unified Framework for Quantum-Gravity Unification

Active Research

My primary research focuses on developing a unified framework that bridges quantum mechanics with gravitational theory through the concept of chronons - discrete units of time at the quantum level. This revolutionary approach challenges conventional understanding of spacetime continuity and offers new perspectives on quantum gravity.

The Chronon-Super Quantum Level Model proposes that time itself is quantized at the Planck scale, providing a mathematical foundation for reconciling the probabilistic nature of quantum mechanics with the deterministic framework of general relativity. This work has led to three peer-reviewed publications and opened new avenues for experimental validation.

By treating time as a quantized variable, we can resolve long-standing paradoxes in quantum gravity and potentially unlock new understandings of black hole thermodynamics, quantum entanglement across spacetime, and the fundamental nature of reality itself.
