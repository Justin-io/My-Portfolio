3. Abstract

3.1 Background & Problem Statement

Modern physics faces unresolved challenges at the intersection of quantum mechanics (QM) and general relativity (GR). At the Planck scale, both theories break down, leaving fundamental questions unanswered: Is time continuous? What lies beyond the Planck barrier? Can spacetime emerge from a deeper structure?

This paper introduces the Chronon-Super Quantum Level (SQL) Model, a novel theoretical framework proposing that:

  • Chronons are the fundamental quanta of time, discrete and independent of energy scales.
  • Space Grains constitute the elementary units of space, forming a granular substrate.
  • The Super Quantum Level is a pre-geometric domain where energy exists in discrete "chunks" smaller than any known quantum.

Time does not flow continuously but emerges from the pulsing activation of Chronons embedded within Space Grains, triggered by an initial disturbance the Big Bang. This model redefines time as a discrete, responsive process, not a background parameter.

The Chronon-SQL Model introduces three foundational elements:
